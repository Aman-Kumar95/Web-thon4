Types of Super-Nova:

Type II :

Type II supernova results from the rapid collapse and violent explosion of a massive star. Type II supernovae are distinguished from other types of supernovae by the presence of hydrogen in their spectra.

Type Ia

Occurs in a binary star system where a white dwarf star accretes material from a companion star. Type Ia supernovae are often used as "standard candles" to measure distances in the universe.

Type Ib and Ic Supernovae

These are subsets of Type II supernovae, Type Ib: Loss of outer hydrogen, but retains helium in the core. Type Ic: Loss of both hydrogen and helium in the outer layers.
